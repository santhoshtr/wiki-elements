<wiki-wikitext> == Violin == The '''violin''', colloquially known as a '''[[fiddle]]''', is a wooden [[chordophone]], and is the smallest, and thus highest-pitched instrument ([[soprano]]) in regular use in the [[violin family]]. Smaller violin-type instruments exist, including the [[violino piccolo]] and the [[pochette (musical instrument)|pochette]], but these are virtually unused. Most violins have a hollow wooden body, and commonly have four [[strings (music)|strings]] (sometimes [[five-string violin|five]]), usually tuned in [[perfect fifth]]s with notes G3, D4, A4, E5, and are most commonly played by drawing a [[bow (music)|bow]] across the strings. [[File:Gaudenzio Ferrari 002.jpg|thumb|The cupola of Madonna dei Miracoli in [[Saronno]], [[Italy]], with angels playing violin, viola, and cello, dates from 1535 and is one of the earliest depictions of the violin family.]] The violin can also be played by plucking the strings with the fingers ([[pizzicato]]) and, in specialized cases, by striking the strings with the wooden side of the bow ([[col legno]]). </wiki-wikitext>
== Violin == The '''violin''', colloquially known as a '''[[fiddle]]''', is a wooden [[chordophone]], and is the smallest, and thus highest-pitched instrument ([[soprano]]) in regular use in the [[violin family]]. Smaller violin-type instruments exist, including the [[violino piccolo]] and the [[pochette (musical instrument)|pochette]], but these are virtually unused. Most violins have a hollow wooden body, and commonly have four [[strings (music)|strings]] (sometimes [[five-string violin|five]]), usually tuned in [[perfect fifth]]s with notes G3, D4, A4, E5, and are most commonly played by drawing a [[bow (music)|bow]] across the strings. [[File:Gaudenzio Ferrari 002.jpg|thumb|The cupola of Madonna dei Miracoli in [[Saronno]], [[Italy]], with angels playing violin, viola, and cello, dates from 1535 and is one of the earliest depictions of the violin family.]] The violin can also be played by plucking the strings with the fingers ([[pizzicato]]) and, in specialized cases, by striking the strings with the wooden side of the bow ([[col legno]]).
